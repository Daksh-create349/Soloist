## 🔥 01 — Problem

The one-person agency is the fastest-growing segment of the creator economy. Solo designers, marketers, developers, and consultants can deliver agency-quality output using AI tools — but they're drowning in operational chaos.

> 💡 The average solo operator uses 12–17 different tools per month and spends 28+ hours a week on non-billable work — chasing invoices, writing proposals, responding to leads, and manually piecing together what their business actually looks like.

### The 5 real problems

- **Tool fragmentation** — CRM in Notion, contracts in Bonsai, invoices in Wave, leads on LinkedIn, trends on Twitter, gigs on Upwork. No system talks to another. Context is always lost.
- **Reactive opportunity discovery** — By the time a solo op sees a trending niche, a job post, or a collab request — it's been up for 3 days. Early movers have already won.
- **No unified business intelligence** — They can't answer "which client type earns me most per hour?" or "is my pipeline healthy?" without manually exporting data from 5 tools into a spreadsheet.
- **Manual operations** — Follow-up emails, client onboarding, project status updates, invoice reminders — all done manually, even when AI tools exist for each step individually.
- **Scaling hits a wall** — At $8–15K/mo, the solo op can't grow further without hiring or drowning. They need an "invisible team" — an AI layer that runs ops while they focus on delivery.

> ⚠️ **Root cause:** Every tool solves one slice of the problem. None of them share context. None of them are proactive. None of them understand that their user is one person running an entire business alone.

---

## ✨ 02 — Solution

Soloist is a **unified AI operating system** built exclusively for solo operators. It's not another CRM. It's not another automation tool. It's the single place where client relationships, business operations, and opportunity pipeline live together — with an AI layer running the connective tissue between them.

| Pillar | Name | What it does |
|--------|------|-------------|
| 01 | Client Hub | Full CRM + contracts + invoicing + comms timeline. AI pre-fills proposals and detects churn risk. |
| 02 | Ops Autopilot | Automates follow-ups, onboarding sequences, status reports, and invoice reminders. Set rules once. |
| 03 | Opportunity Radar | Monitors job boards, LinkedIn, Reddit, and niche communities. Surfaces ranked leads, gigs, collabs, and trends for your specific niche — proactively. |

> ✅ **Key insight:** When the three pillars share the same data layer — your skills, rates, capacity, past clients — the AI can make connections no standalone tool can. "You have 12 hours free next month. Here are 3 leads that match your exact stack and rate. One of them is trending."

## 🏗️ 03 — Core Product Features

### Pillar 1 — Client Hub

- **AI-powered onboarding** — Client fills a questionnaire → AI auto-generates project brief, scope doc, and first invoice draft. From first call to signed contract in under 10 minutes.
- **Unified communication timeline** — All emails, messages, calls, and file exchanges with a client in one scrollable thread — regardless of which channel they came from (Gmail, WhatsApp, LinkedIn).
- **Client health score** — AI monitors engagement signals, payment patterns, and communication frequency to flag at-risk clients before they churn, and identify upsell moments.
- **Smart contracts & e-sign** — Template library + AI customization. E-signatures built in. Contracts auto-link to invoices and project milestones.

### Pillar 2 — Ops Autopilot

- **Automation builder (no-code)** — Visual rule builder: "If client hasn't responded in 3 days → send AI-drafted follow-up." Covers proposals, invoices, check-ins, and offboarding.
- **Auto client reports** — Weekly/monthly status reports auto-generated from project data and sent to clients. Makes you look like a 5-person team.
- **Revenue intelligence dashboard** — Real-time view of MRR, pipeline value, avg. contract size, and profitability per client type. Answers "where is my time worth most?"
- **Capacity planner** — Tracks billable hours vs. availability. When you get a new lead, it tells you whether you can take it on and when.

### Pillar 3 — Opportunity Radar

- **Niche profile engine** — You define your skills, rates, ideal client type, and preferred work style. The AI scores every opportunity it finds — only surfaces what's actually relevant.
- **Multi-source lead monitor** — Watches Upwork, LinkedIn, Reddit, Indie Hackers, Contra, and niche Slack communities for leads, gigs, and collab requests in real time.
- **Trend signals** — "AI product design is up 41% in job posts this week." Surface emerging niches before they're mainstream.
- **Weekly opportunity digest** — Every Monday: a curated, ranked digest of the 10 best gigs, 5 collab opportunities, and 3 trend signals tailored to your profile.
- **One-click apply with AI draft** — For any discovered opportunity, AI auto-drafts a personalized proposal using your profile, past work, and the specific job requirements. Edit and send in 2 minutes.

## 🏰 10 — Defensibility & Moats

- **Data network effects** — The more solo ops use Soloist, the better the opportunity matching algorithm gets. Opportunity ranking improves as the system learns what "good fit" means across thousands of profiles.
- **Switching cost** — Entire client history, contracts, automations, and niche profile lives in Soloist. Switching means starting from zero. Churn drops sharply after 90 days of use.
- **Niche profile depth** — The AI builds a rich model of each user's skills, rates, style, and ideal clients over time. This personalization gap is impossible to replicate without history.
- **Community distribution** — Solo operators are extremely social — they share tools in tight communities. A great product + build-in-public story = viral distribution that money can't easily buy.

01Problem Statement — The Real Pain
The "one-person agency" is the fastest-growing segment of the creator economy. A solo designer, marketer, developer, or consultant can now deliver agency-quality output using AI tools — but they're drowning in operational chaos.

The average solo operator uses 12–17 different tools per month. They spend 28+ hours a week on non-billable work — chasing invoices, writing proposals, responding to leads, and manually piecing together what their business actually looks like. That's time stolen from the work they're actually great at.

The five real problems
01
Tool fragmentation: CRM in Notion, contracts in Bonsai, invoices in Wave, leads on LinkedIn, trends on Twitter, gigs on Upwork. No system talks to another. Context is always lost.
02
Reactive opportunity discovery: By the time a solo op sees a trending niche, a job post, or a collab request — it's been up for 3 days. Early movers have already won.
03
No unified business intelligence: They can't answer "which client type earns me most per hour?" or "is my pipeline healthy?" without manually exporting data from 5 tools into a spreadsheet.
04
Ops are still manual: Follow-up emails, client onboarding, project status updates, invoice reminders — all done manually, even when AI tools exist for each step individually.
05
Scaling hits a wall: At a certain revenue point ($8–15K/mo), the solo op can't grow further without either hiring or drowning. They need an "invisible team" — an AI layer that runs ops while they focus on delivery.
Root cause: Every tool solves one slice of the problem. None of them share context. None of them are proactive. None of them understand that their user is one person running an entire business alone.

02The Solution — What Soloist Is
Soloist is a unified AI operating system built exclusively for solo operators. It's not another CRM. It's not another automation tool. It's the single place where your client relationships, your business operations, and your opportunity pipeline live together — and an AI layer runs the connective tissue between them.

Pillar 01
Client Hub
Full CRM + contracts + invoicing + comms timeline in one view. AI pre-fills proposals and detects churn risk.

Pillar 02
Ops Autopilot
Automates follow-ups, onboarding sequences, status reports, and invoice reminders. You set rules once.

Pillar 03
Opportunity Radar
Monitors job boards, LinkedIn, Reddit, and niche communities. Surfaces ranked leads, gigs, collabs, and trends for your specific niche — proactively.

The key insight: When these three pillars share the same data layer — your skills, your rates, your capacity, your past clients — the AI can make connections no standalone tool can. "You have 12 hours free next month. Here are 3 leads that match your exact stack and rate. One of them is trending."

03Core Product Features — Production Detail
Pillar 1 — Client Hub
◈
AI-Powered Onboarding
Client fills a questionnaire → AI auto-generates project brief, scope doc, and first invoice draft. From first call to signed contract in under 10 minutes.
◈
Unified Communication Timeline
All emails, messages, calls, and file exchanges with a client in one scrollable thread — regardless of which channel they came from (Gmail, WhatsApp, LinkedIn).
◈
Client Health Score
AI monitors engagement signals, payment patterns, and communication frequency to flag at-risk clients before they churn, and identify upsell moments.
◈
Smart Contracts & e-Sign
Template library + AI customization. E-signatures built in. Contracts auto-link to invoices and project milestones.
Pillar 2 — Ops Autopilot
◉
Automation Builder (No-Code)
Visual rule builder: "If client hasn't responded in 3 days → send AI-drafted follow-up." Covers proposals, invoices, check-ins, and offboarding.
◉
Auto Client Reports
Weekly/monthly status reports auto-generated from project data and sent to clients. Makes you look like a 5-person team.
◉
Revenue Intelligence Dashboard
Real-time view of MRR, pipeline value, avg. contract size, and profitability per client type. Answers "where is my time worth most?"
◉
Capacity Planner
Tracks billable hours vs. availability. When you get a new lead, it tells you whether you can take it on and when.
Pillar 3 — Opportunity Radar
◎
Niche Profile Engine
You define your skills, rates, ideal client type, and preferred work style. The AI uses this to score every opportunity it finds — only surfaces what's actually relevant.
◎
Multi-Source Lead Monitor
Watches Upwork, LinkedIn, Reddit, Indie Hackers, Contra, and niche Slack communities for leads, gigs, and collab requests in real time. First-mover advantage built in.
◎
Trend Signals
"AI product design is up 41% in job posts this week." Surface emerging niches before they're mainstream. Helps solo ops pivot their positioning ahead of the curve.
◎
Weekly Opportunity Digest
Every Monday: a curated, ranked digest of the 10 best gigs, 5 collab opportunities, and 3 trend signals tailored to your profile. Delivered to your inbox and in-app.
◍
One-Click Apply with AI Draft
For any discovered opportunity, AI auto-drafts a personalized proposal using your profile, past work, and the specific job requirements. Edit and send in 2 minutes.

10Defensibility & Moats
Data network effects
The more solo ops use Soloist, the better the opportunity matching algorithm gets. Your opportunity ranking improves as the system learns what "good fit" means across thousands of profiles.

Switching cost
Entire client history, contracts, automations, and niche profile lives in Soloist. Switching means starting from zero. Churn drops sharply after 90 days of use.

Niche profile depth
The AI builds a rich model of each user's skills, rates, style, and ideal clients over time. This personalization gap is impossible to replicate without history.

Distribution via community
Solo operators are extremely social — they share tools in tight communities. A great product + build-in-public story = viral distribution that money can't easily buy.

12Risks & Mitigations
Risk	Likelihood	Mitigation
Big players (Notion, HubSpot) copy the concept	Medium	They're built for teams, not solos. Deep niche focus + community loyalty creates a gap they won't fill for 18–24 months. Move fast.
LinkedIn/Upwork block scrapers	High	Build official API integrations first. Maintain scrapers as fallback. Diversify sources (Reddit, Slack communities, Contra, newsletters).
AI API costs eat margins	Medium	Use Claude Haiku for bulk tasks, Sonnet for complex generation. Implement aggressive caching. Gate heavy AI features behind Pro/Elite tiers.
Low free-to-paid conversion	Medium	Free tier is intentionally constrained (3 clients, 5 automations). The limits are hit fast. Value is obvious before the paywall appears.
Solo ops are hard to reach at scale	Low	They're extremely concentrated in Twitter, ProductHunt, Indie Hackers, and niche Slack/Discord communities. High density, low CAC.
